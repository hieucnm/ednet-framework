VASTN Curriculum Working Group — Assessment Framework Discussion
Informal notes / partial transcript, 4 April 2024

Call started around 2pm. Not everyone joined on time.

[Moderator — Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: Okay let's get going, we've only got an hour. Main thing today is finalising the assessment weighting for the joint AI module. Last time we couldn't agree on whether the project component should be 40 or 50 percent.

[Speaker — Intel representative, name unclear from audio]: From industry's perspective we strongly prefer 50 percent project. The whole point of this programme is applied skills. Written exams test memory, not capability.

[Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: Understood. The concern from the academic side — and I'm speaking for HCMUT here as well based on the pre-meeting email — is that 50 percent project creates equity issues for students who have less time outside class. A 40/30/30 split between project, midterm, and final gives more safety net.

[Speaker — Intel representative]: Could we do 45 percent project, 25 midterm, 30 final? Split the difference?

[Dr. Vo Thanh Nam, HCMUT]: That actually works for me. The issue before was the 50 threshold which triggers a different approval process at HCMUT. 45 avoids that.

[Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: So proposal on the table: 45 project, 25 midterm, 30 final. Anyone object? ... Silence taken as agreement. Good.

[Dr. Vo Thanh Nam, HCMUT]: The other thing — the project brief template. It's been sitting in draft since January. Someone needs to own that and get it to a reviewable state before the pilot in September.

[Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: Can RMIT take that on? I can have a draft by May 15th.

[Dr. Vo Thanh Nam, HCMUT]: That works, as long as we have two weeks to review before it goes to the full committee.

[Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: So committee submission by June 1st. I'll note that down.

[Speaker — Intel representative]: One more thing — grading rubric. Who does the final grade if the project involves both a university supervisor and an industry mentor?

[Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: That's a good point we haven't resolved. Let's put it on the agenda for next time. I think it needs a proper policy decision, not a working group call.

[Dr. Vo Thanh Nam, HCMUT]: Agreed. Escalate to the academic committee.

[Prof. Wiremu Tane, RMIT Vietnam]: Alright, I think that's us. Quick summary — assessment split agreed at 45/25/30, RMIT to draft project brief by May 15th for committee submission June 1st, grading rubric policy to go to academic committee. Next meeting TBD.
