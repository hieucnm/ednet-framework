VASTN Emergency Coordination Call — Scholarship Disbursement Issue
Urgent call, 11 June 2024 — partial transcript, audio quality issues noted

[Assoc. Prof. Tran Minh Quang, HCMUT]: I'm going to start because we need to resolve this today. The issue is that fourteen students travelling to NUS next week have not received their scholarship disbursements. Travel is booked. If they don't have funds by Thursday they cannot go.

[Prof. Sarah Chen, NUS]: We sent the transfer request to NUS Finance on May 28th. Our end is done. The delay is not on our side.

[Ms. Le Thi Bich Ngoc, HCMUT Admin]: I checked with HCMUT Finance this morning. They are saying the international transfer requires a sign-off from the Vice Rector for Finance and he has been travelling since June 6th. He returns Thursday.

[Assoc. Prof. Tran Minh Quang, HCMUT]: Thursday is too late. Can someone else at HCMUT authorise?

[Ms. Le Thi Bich Ngoc, HCMUT Admin]: The policy requires Vice Rector level for transfers above 500 million VND. This is 620 million so it falls above the threshold.

[Speaker — unclear, possibly HUST representative]: Can NUS advance the funds and HCMUT reimburse? That would unblock the students.

[Prof. Sarah Chen, NUS]: I would need to check with NUS Finance but I think this is possible for consortium partners. Let me step off the call for five minutes and call them directly.

[Assoc. Prof. Tran Minh Quang, HCMUT]: Please do. While Sarah checks — Ms. Ngoc, can you get a message to the Vice Rector asking for remote authorisation? Even an email approval might be sufficient for Finance to act.

[Ms. Le Thi Bich Ngoc, HCMUT Admin]: I will try. I have his assistant's number.

[Prof. Sarah Chen, NUS — rejoining]: NUS Finance says yes, they can advance up to SGD 50,000 for consortium partners with a reimbursement agreement signed within 30 days. That should cover the fourteen students.

[Assoc. Prof. Tran Minh Quang, HCMUT]: That is excellent news. Ms. Ngoc, please draft the reimbursement commitment letter today and I will sign it. Send to Prof. Chen by close of business.

[Prof. Sarah Chen, NUS]: Received. I will get NUS Finance to process the advance first thing tomorrow morning. Students should have funds by Wednesday.

[Assoc. Prof. Tran Minh Quang, HCMUT]: Good. Also — after this is resolved we need to review the disbursement authorisation policy. A single sign-off point for international transfers is too fragile. I want this on the agenda for the September board meeting.

[Ms. Le Thi Bich Ngoc, HCMUT Admin]: Noted. I will add it.

[Assoc. Prof. Tran Minh Quang, HCMUT]: Thank you everyone. Quick action summary before we close...
