ASEAN University Network Quality Assurance (AUN-QA) Framework — Version 4.0
Summary Overview for Member Institutions

The AUN-QA Framework Version 4.0 represents a significant revision of the regional quality assurance standards applicable to undergraduate and postgraduate programmes offered by member universities across Southeast Asia. The framework is organised into three domains: Programme Outcomes and Curriculum Design, Teaching and Learning Approach, and Programme Quality Enhancement.

Under the Programme Outcomes domain, institutions are required to demonstrate that intended learning outcomes are explicitly mapped to national qualifications frameworks and to the ASEAN Qualifications Reference Framework (AQRF). Each programme must provide documented evidence of at least one complete curriculum mapping cycle, showing how individual course outcomes contribute to programme-level outcomes. The minimum acceptable mapping coverage is 85 percent of programme-level outcomes traceable to at least two course-level outcomes each.

The Teaching and Learning Approach domain introduces new requirements for work-integrated learning components. From the 2025 academic year, all engineering and technology programmes seeking AUN-QA certification must include a minimum of 160 hours of industry-engaged learning per student, defined as structured activities conducted in collaboration with an external partner organisation. Virtual industry engagement activities are acceptable for up to 40 percent of this requirement, provided they involve real project briefs from actual organisations.

The Programme Quality Enhancement domain mandates a structured annual review process. Each programme must convene a programme board that includes at least one external industry representative and one representative from a peer institution outside the home country. The programme board must produce a written annual report addressing graduate employment outcomes, student satisfaction data, and at least two improvement actions implemented since the previous review.

Institutions applying for initial AUN-QA certification under Version 4.0 must submit a self-assessment report using the standardised template published on the AUN-QA portal. The self-assessment must be submitted at least six months before the scheduled site visit. Provisional certification, valid for two years, may be granted where a programme meets at least 70 percent of criteria at the time of assessment, subject to a verified improvement plan for the remaining criteria.

Member institutions are advised to note that Version 4.0 introduces stricter evidence requirements compared to Version 3.0, particularly regarding the traceability of learning outcomes and the documentation of industry partner engagement. Programmes currently certified under Version 3.0 will be required to transition to Version 4.0 standards at their next scheduled renewal, expected to occur on a rolling basis between 2025 and 2027.
