VASTN Annual Progress Report 2023 — Programme Performance Section (Extract)

The Vietnam AI and Semiconductor Training Network completed its first full year of programme delivery in 2023, with results that broadly met the targets established in the founding consortium agreement while revealing several operational challenges requiring attention in the year ahead.

Total enrolment across the two flagship programmes — the Joint AI Engineering Certificate and the Semiconductor Design Fundamentals Short Course — reached 187 students, representing 94 percent of the combined intake target of 200. The shortfall was concentrated in the Semiconductor Design programme, where enrolment reached only 61 percent of target due to delays in establishing the simulation laboratory infrastructure at one partner institution. The AI Engineering Certificate programme exceeded its intake target, enrolling 142 students against a target of 120.

Academic performance outcomes were positive. The overall pass rate across both programmes was 88.3 percent, exceeding the 85 percent target set in the consortium agreement. Student satisfaction surveys, administered at the end of each module, returned a mean satisfaction score of 3.9 out of 5.0 across all institutions and modules, with the highest scores recorded for the industry-delivered modules and the lowest for the mathematics foundation module, which several students described as insufficiently contextualised for engineering application.

Industry engagement exceeded expectations. Eleven partner companies participated in the programme during 2023, contributing guest lectures, capstone project briefs, and internship placements. A total of 34 students completed industry internships of eight weeks or longer, with 9 receiving full-time employment offers from their host organisations before graduation. Intel Vietnam and Bosch Vietnam were the most active industry partners, each contributing over 80 hours of teaching and mentoring time.

Operational challenges included delays in procurement of licensed design software, inconsistencies in the credit recognition procedures across partner universities, and a higher-than-anticipated administrative burden associated with managing student mobility between institutions. The network secretariat has proposed three remediation measures to the steering committee: a centralised procurement mechanism for shared software licences, a joint credit framework to be submitted to the Ministry of Education and Training for endorsement, and a dedicated student mobility coordinator position to be funded from the 2024 programme budget.

Financial performance was within 4 percent of the approved budget. The main variance was an underspend in the travel and accommodation category, attributable to two planned in-person workshops being converted to virtual format due to scheduling conflicts. The underspent funds have been carried forward to support expanded industry engagement activities in 2024.
