VASTN Student Mobility Outcomes Report — Cohort 2023
Prepared by: Student Affairs Working Group, February 2024

Executive Summary
This report documents the academic, professional, and personal development outcomes of the 34 VASTN students who completed an outbound mobility placement of eight weeks or longer during the 2023 academic year. Data were collected through end-of-placement surveys, institutional transcript records, and structured follow-up interviews conducted three months after placement completion.

Academic Outcomes
Of the 34 mobility students, 31 completed their placement successfully and returned to their home institution. Two students extended their placements at the request of their host organisation and are expected to complete by March 2024. One student withdrew for personal reasons unrelated to the placement. The mean academic grade for placement-related assessments was 76.4 out of 100, marginally above the cohort average of 73.1 for non-mobility students in the same semester.

Credit recognition was completed without issue for 28 of the 31 returning students. For three students, delays in transcript issuance by the host institution meant that credit recognition was not finalised before the home institution's grade submission deadline; these cases were resolved through a provisional credit arrangement and subsequently confirmed.

Professional Development Outcomes
Post-placement surveys indicate that 91 percent of students reported improved confidence in applying technical knowledge in a professional setting. Specific competencies rated most improved included: working in cross-cultural teams (88 percent), communicating technical findings to non-specialist audiences (79 percent), and using industry-standard tools not covered in the home institution curriculum (74 percent).

Nine students received full-time employment offers from their host organisation before completing their degree. All nine accepted the offers, conditional on graduation. Seven of these placements were with Intel Vietnam, one with Bosch Vietnam, and one with a local technology startup that had participated in a VASTN-sponsored innovation challenge.

Challenges Identified
Three recurring challenges were identified from student and supervisor feedback. First, pre-departure preparation was considered insufficient by 44 percent of students, who felt they lacked adequate context about workplace norms and expectations at their host organisation. Second, mid-placement check-ins between home institution supervisors and students were inconsistent, with 38 percent of students reporting no contact with their home supervisor during the placement. Third, the reimbursement process for student travel and living expenses was described as slow and administratively burdensome by 62 percent of students.

Recommendations
The working group recommends three actions for the 2024 mobility cohort: introducing a mandatory two-day pre-departure orientation programme; establishing a fortnightly check-in protocol between home supervisors and mobility students; and streamlining the reimbursement process by pre-loading a fixed daily allowance onto a network-issued payment card rather than requiring students to claim expenses retrospectively.
