Memorandum of Understanding between HCMUT and NUS — Joint Research and Education Programme
Signed: 14 January 2024 (Extract — Articles 3 through 7)

Article 3 — Scope of Collaboration
The parties agree to collaborate in the following areas: (a) joint supervision of postgraduate research students, with a minimum of two jointly supervised doctoral candidates per year; (b) curriculum co-development for the VASTN Joint AI Engineering Certificate, with each party contributing no less than three modules per academic year; (c) reciprocal short-term faculty exchanges of one to four weeks duration, with a target of four exchanges per year between the two institutions; and (d) joint applications for external research funding, with HCMUT designated as lead applicant for Vietnamese government funding schemes and NUS as lead applicant for Singapore-based and international funding schemes.

Article 4 — Intellectual Property
Intellectual property created jointly through activities under this MOU shall be jointly owned by both parties in equal shares unless otherwise agreed in writing before the commencement of the relevant activity. Each party retains sole ownership of intellectual property created independently before or during the MOU period without material contribution from the other party. Publications arising from joint research may proceed after a mutual review period not exceeding 45 days, during which either party may request removal of commercially sensitive information. Neither party may file a patent application on jointly developed technology without the written consent of the other.

Article 5 — Financial Arrangements
Each party shall bear its own costs for activities under this MOU unless a specific cost-sharing arrangement is agreed in writing for a particular activity. Student exchange mobility grants, where available, shall be applied for jointly by the parties and allocated according to the terms of the relevant funding programme.

Article 6 — Student Mobility
Students participating in exchange activities under this MOU shall be subject to the academic and administrative regulations of the host institution during their period of study. The home institution shall accept credits earned at the host institution for modules that have been pre-approved through the joint curriculum committee. The maximum credit transfer in any semester shall not exceed 18 credit units at NUS or the equivalent at HCMUT.

Article 7 — Duration and Termination
This MOU shall remain in force for a period of five years from the date of signing, subject to annual review by the Joint Coordination Committee. Either party may terminate this MOU with six months' written notice. Obligations arising from activities commenced before the notice of termination shall continue until those activities are concluded.
