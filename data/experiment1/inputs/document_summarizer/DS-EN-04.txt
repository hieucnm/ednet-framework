Briefing Note — Erasmus+ Capacity Building Call 2024
Prepared by: VASTN Secretariat, 5 March 2024
For: VASTN Steering Committee members

The European Commission published the 2024 call for Erasmus+ Capacity Building in Higher Education projects on 24 January 2024. The application deadline is 5 June 2024. This briefing summarises the key features of the call and assesses the fit with VASTN's current priorities.

The Capacity Building strand funds projects that strengthen the quality, relevance, and modernisation of higher education in partner countries outside the EU. For the 2024 call, Southeast Asia is a priority region. Projects must involve a minimum of two EU institutions and two non-EU partner country institutions. VASTN, with members in Vietnam and Singapore, would need to recruit at least two EU university partners to be eligible.

Project grants range from EUR 400,000 to EUR 800,000 over three years. Eligible costs include staff costs, travel, equipment, and indirect costs at a flat rate of 7 percent of direct costs. The Commission requires that at least 70 percent of the grant be spent in partner countries. There is no matching funding requirement, but institutions are expected to demonstrate that project outputs will be sustained after the grant period ends.

Relevance assessment: The 2024 call has three thematic priorities — digital transformation, green transition, and inclusive education. VASTN's work on AI and semiconductor education aligns clearly with the digital transformation priority. The network's current gap in formal quality assurance certification (AUN-QA is in progress but not yet complete) may be presented as a project output rather than a prerequisite.

Recommended action: The secretariat recommends that the steering committee authorise an expression of interest to two shortlisted EU partners — TU Delft (Netherlands) and KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Sweden) — by 31 March 2024. If both confirm interest, the secretariat will coordinate preparation of the full application with a target submission date of 20 May 2024, leaving two weeks of buffer before the deadline.
