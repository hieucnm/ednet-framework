## Curriculum Framework: Master of Engineering in AI Systems — Joint Degree Programme

**Participating institutions:** Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HCMUT), National University of Singapore (NUS), Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST)

**Program goals:** This programme delivers a research-informed postgraduate degree that integrates AI engineering depth with semiconductor systems knowledge, equipping graduates to lead applied AI research and advanced engineering development across both industry and academic contexts. The curriculum is designed to bridge foundational AI theory with hardware-aware systems engineering, reflecting the convergence of AI workloads and specialised silicon architectures in contemporary technology development. Beyond individual graduate outcomes, the programme establishes a reusable joint degree infrastructure — including shared governance protocols, credit transfer mechanisms, and co-supervision frameworks — that can serve as a template for future collaborative programmes across VASTN member institutions. All instruction is delivered in English to ensure accessibility for international students and to prepare graduates for global professional environments.

**Target audience:** The programme is designed for students holding a bachelor's degree in computer engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, or a closely related discipline, with a minimum cumulative GPA equivalent to 7.0 out of 10 (or recognised international equivalent). Both domestic and international applicants are eligible. The programme specifically targets individuals seeking careers in AI research, semiconductor design, and AI systems engineering, including those transitioning from industry into research-oriented roles and recent graduates pursuing advanced technical specialisation.

**Total duration:** 2 years full-time (4 semesters), with Semesters 1–2 delivered at HCMUT, Semester 3 delivered at NUS (with a parallel track option at HUST subject to partner agreement), and Semester 4 dedicated to joint thesis research supervised across all three institutions.

### Assessment Approach

Assessment across the programme is designed to be continuous, research-aligned, and consistent with the academic standards of all three participating institutions. Each module employs a combination of formative and summative assessment instruments calibrated to its learning outcomes and modality.

For foundational and technical modules (M01–M04, M06–M07, M09–M10), assessment typically combines written examinations or timed problem sets with practical assignments, laboratory reports, or implementation projects. This dual approach ensures both conceptual understanding and applied engineering competence are evaluated. Grading rubrics for all shared modules are jointly developed and approved by academic representatives from all three institutions to ensure equivalence of standards across sites.

M05 (Research Methods and Academic Communication) is assessed through a structured literature review, a research proposal presentation, and a peer review exercise, reflecting the scholarly practices students will employ throughout their thesis work.

M08 (Advanced Topics in AI Systems) is assessed through a seminar presentation and a critical synthesis paper, requiring students to engage with primary research literature and demonstrate the ability to situate emerging work within broader technical and societal contexts.

M11 (Industry Practicum or Research Lab Rotation) is assessed through a structured technical report and a supervisor evaluation form completed by the industry or laboratory host. Assessment criteria are standardised across all placement sites and reviewed by the programme's joint academic committee.

Elective modules (M12, M13) are assessed according to the host institution's standard assessment practices for those courses, provided that learning outcomes and assessment criteria are disclosed to students in advance and are consistent with the programme's overall standards. [PARTNER REVIEW REQUIRED]

M14 (Joint Thesis Research) is the capstone assessment of the programme. Students produce a written thesis evaluated by an examination panel comprising at least one supervisor from each of the two or more institutions involved in co-supervision, plus one independent examiner. The thesis defence is conducted in English and may be held in person or via videoconference to accommodate cross-institutional participation. Thesis assessment criteria — including originality, technical rigour, clarity of exposition, and capacity for independent scholarship — are jointly defined and documented in the programme handbook. The thesis must satisfy the examination requirements of all three institutions for the joint degree to be conferred. [PARTNER REVIEW REQUIRED]

Grade conversion and transcript notation protocols are established in the inter-institutional agreement to ensure that credits and grades earned at each site are accurately represented on all three institutions' official transcripts.
