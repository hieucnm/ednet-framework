## Curriculum Framework: Applied Generative AI for Engineers
**Participating institutions:** HCMUT, NUS, Intel Vietnam  
**Program goals:** Equip students with practical skills to design, fine-tune, evaluate, and deploy generative AI systems in engineering contexts; develop understanding of responsible AI principles and their application in product development; bridge academic foundations with industry workflows used at semiconductor and software companies  
**Target audience:** Final-year undergraduate and first-year postgraduate students in computer engineering, software engineering, and electrical engineering; students should have completed at least one prior machine learning course  
**Total duration:** 12 weeks, 3 contact hours per week  

### Module Structure
| Module | Title | Learning Outcomes | Credits | Modality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| M01 | Introduction to Generative AI | Understand the basics of generative AI and its applications in engineering | 0.5 | Online |
| M02 | Designing Generative Models | Gain skills in designing generative models for engineering tasks | 0.5 | Hybrid |
| M03 | Fine-tuning and Evaluation | Learn techniques for fine-tuning and evaluating generative models | 0.5 | Hybrid |
| M04 | Deployment in Engineering Contexts | Deploy generative AI systems in real-world engineering scenarios | 0.5 | In-person |
| M05 | Responsible AI Principles | Understand and apply responsible AI principles in product development | 0.5 | Online |
| M06 | Industry Applications and Workflows | Experience industry workflows and applications of generative AI | 0.5 | In-person |

### Assessment Approach
The assessment strategy will include a combination of individual assignments, group projects, and practical labs. Each module will have a formative assessment component to ensure ongoing feedback and learning. The final assessment will involve a capstone project where students design and deploy a generative AI system, demonstrating their understanding of both technical and ethical considerations.

### Collaboration Mechanisms Between Institutions
HCMUT will host in-person sessions and coordinate local logistics, while NUS will provide online course materials and virtual lectures. Intel Vietnam will deliver industry-led sessions, focusing on practical applications and case studies. All institutions will collaborate on the development of course content and assessment criteria to ensure alignment with academic and industry standards.
