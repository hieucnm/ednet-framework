## Summary
**Document type:** Report
**Source language:** English

### Key Points
The VASTN Student Affairs Working Group report covers outcomes for 34 students who completed outbound mobility placements of eight weeks or more during the 2023 academic year. Academic performance was slightly above the non-mobility cohort average, and credit recognition was completed smoothly for most returning students, with three cases resolved through provisional arrangements. A large majority of students reported gains in professional confidence, cross-cultural teamwork, and technical communication skills, and nine students received conditional employment offers from host organisations. Three notable challenges emerged: inadequate pre-departure preparation, inconsistent mid-placement contact with home supervisors, and a slow expense reimbursement process. The working group proposes a mandatory pre-departure orientation, a structured fortnightly check-in protocol, and a pre-loaded daily allowance card to replace retrospective expense claims.

### Implications for the Network
These findings point to specific gaps in student support infrastructure that affect the quality and consistency of mobility experiences across member institutions. Adoption of the proposed measures would require coordinated action from both home institutions and network administration.

### Recommended Follow-up
Member institutions should confirm readiness to implement the three recommended changes ahead of the 2024 mobility cohort intake.
