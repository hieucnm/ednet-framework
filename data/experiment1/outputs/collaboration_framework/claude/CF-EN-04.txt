## Collaboration Framework Outline

**Parties:** Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HCMUT); RMIT University Vietnam

**Purpose:** This framework establishes a short-term student mobility arrangement enabling final-year undergraduate students from each institution to complete elective modules at the partner institution. The arrangement operates on a basis of mutual credit recognition and is designed to advance the interdisciplinary education objectives of the Vietnam–Australia Science and Technology Network (VASTN). It does not constitute, and shall not be construed as, a formal joint degree or double degree programme.

**Effective period:** Two (2) academic years: 2024–2025 and 2025–2026, renewable by mutual written agreement prior to expiry.

---

### Scope of Collaboration

The collaboration encompasses reciprocal short-term student mobility at the undergraduate level, limited to final-year students seeking enrolment in elective modules at the partner institution. Core activities include: the reciprocal acceptance of exchange students each semester; the maintenance of a jointly agreed list of eligible elective modules; mutual credit recognition for successfully completed modules; coordinated orientation programming for incoming students; and structured end-of-programme review between designated institutional coordinators. All activities are framed within the broader VASTN interdisciplinary education agenda and are subject to the academic regulations of the respective host institution.

### Resource Sharing Arrangements

Tuition fees are waived on a reciprocal basis under this arrangement: each institution waives tuition fees for incoming exchange students from the partner institution, and students pay tuition fees solely to their home institution for the relevant semester. No additional institutional fees are charged by the host institution in connection with elective module enrolment under this framework. [LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]

Accommodation and living costs are borne entirely by individual students and are not covered by either institution under this arrangement. Neither institution is obligated to provide subsidised housing; however, each party is encouraged to share information about available accommodation options with incoming exchange students as part of the orientation process.

No further resource commitments (including staffing, infrastructure, or financial transfers between institutions) have been specified under this framework. Any such arrangements, should they be required, are to be determined by mutual agreement. [LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]

### Governance and Dispute Resolution [LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]

**Decision-making authority:** Day-to-day administration of the mobility arrangement shall be managed by a designated Student Affairs Coordinator at each institution. Decisions requiring institutional commitment — including amendments to the eligible module list, changes to student intake numbers, and renewal or termination of the framework — shall require written agreement from authorised representatives of both institutions.

**Academic disputes:** Any academic dispute arising from a student's participation in elective modules at the host institution shall be addressed in the first instance under the host institution's applicable academic regulations and student grievance procedures.

**Joint Academic Committee:** Where a student exercises the right of appeal beyond the host institution's standard procedures, the matter shall be referred to a Joint Academic Committee comprising designated academic representatives from both HCMUT and RMIT University Vietnam. The composition, quorum, and operating procedures of this Joint Academic Committee are to be formally established and documented by both parties prior to the commencement of the first intake. [LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]

**Framework-level disputes:** Any disagreement between the two institutions regarding the interpretation or implementation of this framework shall be addressed through good-faith consultation between senior representatives of both parties before any formal escalation.

### Review and Renewal Conditions

A formal end-of-programme review shall be conducted by the Student Affairs Coordinators of both institutions at the conclusion of each academic year (i.e., at the end of 2024–2025 and again at the end of 2025–2026). This review shall assess student participation rates, credit recognition outcomes, module list adequacy, orientation effectiveness, and any disputes or operational issues encountered during the period.

The eligible elective module list shall be reviewed and reconfirmed or updated annually, prior to the commencement of each academic year, by agreement between both parties.

Renewal of the framework beyond the 2025–2026 academic year is subject to mutual written agreement by both institutions, informed by the findings of the end-of-programme reviews. Either party may propose amendments to the terms of the framework as a condition of renewal. Should either party elect not to renew, written notice should be provided to the other party no later than three (3) months before the expiry of the current effective period, to allow for appropriate student transition arrangements.
