## Collaboration Framework Outline
**Parties:** VASTN Consortium (HCMUT, HUST, NUS, RMIT Vietnam), TU Delft (Netherlands), KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Sweden)

**Purpose:** Establish a project collaboration framework for a proposed Erasmus+ Capacity Building in Higher Education application focused on strengthening AI and semiconductor engineering education in Vietnam through curriculum modernisation, staff development, and quality assurance capacity building.

**Effective period:** 3 years, aligned with Erasmus+ project grant cycle, commencing upon grant award (estimated Q1 2025)

### Scope of Collaboration
The collaboration will focus on joint curriculum development for two new AI engineering modules compliant with AUN-QA Version 4.0 standards, conducting three staff development workshops hosted alternately at TU Delft, KTH, and HCMUT, establishing a quality assurance peer review mechanism between VASTN and EU partner institutions, facilitating a student short-term exchange programme of two to four weeks for up to 20 VASTN students per year at TU Delft or KTH, and the joint publication of a best-practice guide on industry-integrated AI engineering education in Southeast Asia.

The decision-making authority will reside with a Steering Committee comprising representatives from each institution, chaired by HCMUT as the lead applicant. Dispute resolution will follow a structured process involving mediation by the Steering Committee, with escalation to an independent arbitrator if necessary.

### Review and Renewal Conditions
The collaboration framework will be reviewed annually, with a comprehensive evaluation at the end of the three-year period. Continuation of activities beyond the grant period will be subject to a separate sustainability plan to be agreed upon by all parties before the project's end.
